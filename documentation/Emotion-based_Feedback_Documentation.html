  Introduction

  Purpose

  Our collective acknowledgment of the importance of having a tool that gives real and accurate feedback led us in the decision for the creation of this application. The main goal is to bring closer together the people who need feedback and the people willing to provide it. We also want to reduce the time spend reading all the reviews, by interpreting the selected emotions beforehand, easing the product providers' process of creating a bigger picture of the customers' emotions and opinions regarding the displayed product, service, location or even person.

  Intended Audience and Reading Suggestions

  The intended audience of this paper are developers, testers, project managers, marketing staff, users and any other category that is interested in developing or using our application. In case of inexperienced users who want to know more about the ways of using our application, we recommend our Overall Description, User Interfaces, System Features, as well as any other section that raises interest, starting with the Introduction, Overall Description, External Interface Requirements and finishing off with Further Development .
 We will generally address any category that can be uploaded and reviewed, being a service, person, event, object etc. as a product.

  Product Scope

  Our initiative is to bring together any type of provider and their clients. By doing so, the time spent by the providers of goods is exponentially reduced and they have a clearer picture of their customers' opinions on their service. It is useful especially for people who want to launch a new product and who do not have the resources to analyse the market on their own before taking the big step. Our goal is also to create a safe place, accessible to anyone who wants to share their experience and emotions on a product.

  Overall Description

  Product Perspective

  Our application checks both the functional and quality requirements for products of its kind, so we will dig deeper into some of its most important characteristics, which will be detailed in the sections below.
  The functional requirements that define its behaviour are the possibility of creating an account, if not existent, leaving room for users that are not logged to use the application in a similar manner to logged reviewers. However, the functionality that describes uploading products as forms that expire, as well as the interpreted results, is only available to the users that already have an account.
 The application also fits quality requirements, as it is precise in generating information, highly throughput and exceedingly safe for both registered users and guests, in terms of confidentiality and data management.
  The problem which we identified is the well known need of business owners and management teams, as well as artists and regular people to centralise and analyse information about their specific product or provided service. These features shift the product perspective as a powerful tool that can significantly reduce, or even fully dismiss the time spent researching. As users leave anonymous reviews, the level of honesty is increased and the application’s analysis of the reviews are be indubitably accurate.
 The evaluated emotions are selected from the Plutchik’s Wheel of Emotions, shown below. Each "petal" represents an emotion, while the closer to the center the emotion is, the more intense it is felt.



  Product Functions

 We will split the functionalities into more subcategories, as follows:

 Logged Users Functions

Uploading a form as a “post” and request reviews from any category of people that are willing to help, or are interested in giving their review, in case they personally benefited from its usage, or from the provided service, such as events, firms, sole proprietorship's and any other kind of service providers.
 2. Set an expiration date for the uploaded form and wait for the reviews to pile up. Once the expiration date has passed, a graphical analysis of the messages is provided by the application, that consists of elegantly structured data, that sums up the emotions chosen by the reviewers when sending feedback on the characteristics imposed by the owner. The form uploaders are also able to see the emotions inserted by the reviewers for each aspect they chose.
3. See personalised suggestions on products they might also want to review.
 4. Access their personal data and update any information about themselves.

 Unlogged Users Functions

1. Creating an account.
 2. Anyone can access the site and leave a review, and only their responses are stored, which makes the process fully anonymous. For users that have an account, however, the message is linked to their information. The data is not publicly accessible, as it is only used by the application in order to better detect the patterns of behaviour each client has, and provide a better analysis of the feedback, for the product uploaders.
 3. See what products are trending.

  Software Interfaces

1. Client (Frontend)

 The project will be sectioned by pages and the full structure can be found in our Github Repository, as well as in the Use Case Diagram, and Figma Project, to get a better understanding of the pages' flow. The main structure consists of three folders, each containing a different format of files, HTML, CSS and Javascript. Each HTML file has a personal CSS file, while Javascript files are separated by the type of functionalities they have, such as repeating code, validating input data, or formatting information from files to be shown in the HTML divisions. The following types of files will put together the client side of the application, which is in Node.js:
  • HTML as markup in order to implement the structure and basic features.
  • CSS for implementing the style and for adding features where HTML is limited, such as grids and divisions classes, in order to assure the responsiveness.
  • Javascript used to implement important functions that replicate sections of code and to display the needed information about the account, if logged, the displayed products, as well as to validate basic structures of information on input, an example being the email-input section at signup. The Json files will also be received here in the needed format, in order to create the duplicated HTML divisions with products information.
2. Services and Server (Backend)

  • Node.js as runtime environment for JavaScript code, to create the server and run the web application outside the browser and create the API. As it is non-blocking and single threaded, it is highly scalable.
    The requests differ from multi-threaded servers, as there is no process where the thread is being picked up from the thread pool, and then each thread runs independently. This implies that for each request, no data sharing between the threads is needed. There will be seven main services that will interact with the database, the register, login, products, forms, user-info, statistics , database-monitoring.
3. Communication

 The communication between the services, frontend and the database will be done using Json files mainly, as there will be information such as forms, account and product data, which will be stored and passed with ease.
Although the used server can also initiate the request given its nature, the request-response format is used uni-directionaly for our project, as the interaction is conditioned by the forms that are passed from the client.
The connection will be ensured by pooling, which have TCP Keep-Alive enabled for them, although new connections might be required. More on the connection and communication implementation here.
The communication between the server and the services will be ensured by the following usage of the HTTP methods.
4. Database

Its JSON type documents favor the extraction and insertion of files, as it is facile to use with Node.js applications.   Communication Interfaces

  The application, and mostly the services, have the following communication requirements, in order to work properly:
  • Connection and constant communication with the non-relational database, in order to extract and upload data.
  • Access to the network while running, as it requires a browser.
  • Using HTTP, the server will send HTTP requests with JSON body to the services, while the services will respond with static files, such as images, and JSON-formatted files, for any other kind of information extracted from the database, with the methods: GET, PUT, POST, PATCH and DELETE. In order to support the full spectrum of possible HTTP applications, the Node.js HTTP API is very low-level. This means that the communication will be done with stream handling and message parsing only, with REST constrains.
    The Server is running on PORT 3000, while the available services are: register (PORT 3001), login (PORT 3002),products (PORT 3003), forms (PORT 3004), statistics (PORT 3005), user-information (PORT 3006), database-monitoring (PORT 3007).
  The application is structured in one main component, the Server and seven other services that interact with the database. To ensure security, all interactions with the database are first verified with a token, created by the login service and sent from the Client, and if the user does not have the permission to access, then the request will be denied.
 Also, for security concerns, only generic errors will be displayed, as any error will be treated internally and no exceptions messages will be accessible to the Client. This assures that the services protect the database and the chances of a successfull attack from fake clients, or from malicious data input are significantly decreased.

 Register - will create the account and store the hashed-coded password and other user information in the database.
 Login - will create the token used by the user to log in their account.
 Products - will manage the extraction of information about the products, as well as insert new products.
 Forms - provides the information about the fields selected by the user, and inserts in the database the reviews.
 Statistics - will provide the data that is parsed and used in the Client, to create charts.
 User-information - extracts information about the user, for the "My Account" page, as well as updates information, in case the user wants to change it.
 Database-monitoring - given the fact that the application shall not have any kind of restriction, the "administrator" is an independent service, that will close forms when it's their due date, and clean up any information that is no longer valuable.

  System Features

 Below is the Use Case diagram:

  Create an account

Priority: High.
Stimulus: Once the user is on the Homepage, if they want to add a product, having an account is mandatory. By clicking on the "Sign Up" button and providing an username, email, age and password, the information is sent and inserted in the database. It will be accessed everytime the user wants to log in, or new statistics are requested.
Functional Requirements: In case the process has an error, the user can refresh the page safely and try inserting the information again. The invalid inputs will also create an error, such as wrongly formatted emails from the JavaScript functions, in which case, the process will hold, and the user has to type a correct format. The information and the encrypted password must be sent to the database and stored in order to be validated at the next login process.

  Login

Priority: High.
Stimulus: In order to access any of the functionalities presented above, at the "Creating an account" section, the user must also be able to access their account again.
Functional Requirements: In case of an error, the user can refresh the page safely and try inserting the information again. Just as above, the invalid inputs will create an error, and the user is requested to type a correct format. The token and other information must then be passed and validated, such that some functionalities are limited to different types of users.

 Adding a new product

Priority: High.
Stimulus: After possessing an account, the user will be able to add a new product by accessing the top button. A new form is displayed and the uploader can choose the category and basic points to be reviewed, and add new features that are specific to their product. Adding a deadline is mandatory. A picture which will be encoded in Base64, as well as a basic description can be provided, together with the date when the form shall close.
Functional Requirements: If an error occurs, the inserted information about the product will be lost and must be inserted again. File containing information about the product, and it's link to the uploader account will then be validated and sent to the services, where it will be inserted in the database.

  Reviewing product

Priority: High.
Stimulus: By accessing the full list of products from a category, or by scrolling through the popular, or "For You" sections and pressing the button displayed for adding a review, a new page with a form will open. The form will be generated based on the information in the database, as the uploader chose which aspects to be reviewed.
Functional Requirements: In case of interruptions, the form will not be saved. The file containing all the reviewed aspects, as well as the users data in case they are logged is then stored in the database and the information is used for the statistics of the full category the product belongs to.

  Manage products

Priority: Medium.
Stimulus: The uploaders can access the list of products on their account. The ongoing forms and the ones that have reached their deadline have different functionalities. The ongoing ones can be edited or deleted, while the finalised ones can only provide the files containing the statistics, extracted from the database, interpreted and formatted in a service.
Functional Requirements: If the deletion of a product or closing of a form is done, the product will change state, or be deleted.

  See statistics

Priority: Medium.
Stimulus: On the users personal page, they can access the product forms that have been closed. The statistics will provide information about the other products from the same category, such that the user can get a better idea about how the selected aspects of the uploaded products have been perceived by other users.
Functional Requirements: If the button is pressed, the available formats must be HTML, for engaging data, JSON and CSV, downloadable or not.

  Update account information

Priority: Low.
Stimulus: By accessing the editing page, users can update, as the information will then be sent and updated in the database.
Functional Requirements: In case of error, the unmodified information, before editing, is being kept.

  Search for a desired product

Priority: Low.
Stimulus: By accessing the side menu, the products will be displayed and the user will be able to choose what product they want to review.
Functional Requirements: The full list of products must be displayed, by most recent, or by the most reviewed.




  Further Development Strategies

 The project can be further developed by creating a descriptive review of products, such that using NLP, the emotion that is felt can be interpreted directly from the fully written review. Other special features can be added as well, such as seeing overall ratings for products, in order to create a top with the most liked or disliked products. A new type of account can be developed, for "investors", who could see the top ratings presented above, in order to find products that are worth sponsoring, with more detailed information about the whole market. The admin fearure, not only a service, will also be necessary given the increasing number of account types.
  Given the project's structure and implementation, the ground for improvement has been already prepared, therefore, the source can be further developed, not modified, straightforwardly.
